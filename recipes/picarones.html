Picarones (Chilean Pumpkin Doughnuts)

Picarones are a traditional Chilean dessert made from a soft dough that combines pumpkin or sweet potato with flour and yeast, creating a light and airy texture. These doughnuts are deep-fried until golden and crispy on the outside while remaining soft inside. They are then drizzled with a sweet syrup made from chancaca (raw cane sugar), cinnamon, cloves, and orange peel, adding a rich caramel-like flavor with warm, spiced notes. Their unique taste and texture make them a favorite treat, especially in autumn and winter.

Originally inspired by Spanish buñuelos, picarones have become a beloved part of Chilean cuisine and are commonly enjoyed during national celebrations, fairs, and family gatherings. They are often served as a comforting street food, perfect for pairing with a cup of tea or coffee. With their crispy exterior, fluffy center, and delicious syrup, picarones offer a delightful combination of flavors that make them an irresistible dessert.

Ingredients

  • Dough
    • 1 cup cooked and mashed pumpkin or sweet potatos
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon baking powder
    • ½ teaspoon salt
    • 1 tablespoon sugar/li>
    • ½ cup warm water
    • 1 teaspoon dry yeast
  • Frying
    • Vegetable oil (for deep frying)
  • Syrup
    • 1 cup brown sugar or chancaca (raw cane sugar)
    • 1 cup water
    • 1 cinnamon stick
    • 2 cloves
    • 1 orange peel

Steps to Prepare

  1. Prepare the Dough - In a bowl, mix warm water and yeast. Let sit for 5 minutes. In another bowl, combine flour, baking powder, salt, sugar, and mashed pumpkin. Gradually add the yeast mixture, kneading until a smooth dough forms. Let it rest for 1 hour until it doubles in size.
  2. Make the Syrup - In a saucepan, combine sugar, water, cinnamon, cloves, and orange peel. Simmer for 10–15 minutes until thickened.
  3. Shape and Fry - Heat oil in a deep pan. Wet your hands, take small portions of dough, and shape them into rings. Fry in hot oil until golden brown, flipping once.
  4. Drain and Serve - Remove from oil and place on paper towels to remove excess grease. Serve warm, drizzled with the syrup.
